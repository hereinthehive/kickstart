# Screenplay

A feature screenplay being written in Markdown. Character descriptions and story structure are in place; scenes are being built toward a first draft.

## Files

- Character descriptions and story structure live as Markdown files in this folder.
- Scene files will be added here as writing progresses.
- `tracker.md` — running log of character appearances and scene tones, updated with `/track`.

## Talking to me

You don't need slash commands — just say what you want:

- *"Update the tracker"* or *"Log that scene"* — record which characters appeared, what they did, and whether the scene ran positive or negative for each. (`/track`)
- *"Give me notes"* or *"Review what I have"* — feedback on pages or scenes from inside the project. (`/review`)
- *"Run coverage"* or *"Give me a reader's view"* — formal studio coverage: logline, synopsis, character breakdown, story notes, and a recommendation. (`/coverage`)
- *"Save a checkpoint"* — bookmark this exact state to come back to. (`/checkpoint`)
- *"Undo that"* — reverse the last thing I changed. (`/undo`)
- *"Remember to..."* — save a preference across sessions. (`/remember`)
- *"Wrap up"* — recap this session for next time. (`/wrap`)
- *"What can you do?"* — see this list anytime. (`/help-me`)

## Constraints

- Never rewrite dialogue without being asked.
- Never change the structure document without being asked.
- Don't condense or paraphrase scene drafts — preserve the writer's voice.
- Treat character description files as reference; don't alter them.
